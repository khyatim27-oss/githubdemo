üåé Earthquake

An earthquake is the shaking of the Earth's surface due to tectonic movements.

  • Drop, Cover, and Hold:
    • Drop to your hands and knees, take cover under a sturdy table or desk, and hold on to it until the shaking stops.
  • Stay Indoors:
    • Remain inside if you are already there, away from windows, fireplaces, and heavy, unsecured furniture.
  • Move to open space after shaking stops.
  • Protect Head and Neck:
    • If no shelter is nearby, cover your head and neck with your arms.
  • If in Bed:
    • Stay there, turn face down, and protect your head and neck with a pillow.
  • If Driving:
    • Pull over to a clear, safe location, avoid stopping under bridges or power lines, and stay inside the vehicle.
  • Do Not Use Elevators:
    • Never use lifts during or immediately after a tremor.

üåä Flood

A flood happens when excess water covers normally dry land.

  • Seal Basements:
    • Use waterproofing compounds to seal basement walls.
  • Clear Drains:
    • Keep gutters and drains free of debris.
  • Emergency Kit:
    • Prepare a kit with food, water, flashlights, radio, and medicine.
  • Turn Around, Don't Drown:
    • Never walk, swim, or drive through floodwater; 6 inches can knock you down, and 2 feet can sweep cars away.
  • Evacuate Immediately:
    • Follow instructions from local authorities to evacuate.
  • Elevate Belongings:
    • Move important items to higher ground to avoid flood damage.
  • Shut Off Utilities:
    • Turn off electricity, gas, and water if instructed to do so.

üå™ Cyclone

A cyclone is a powerful rotating storm with strong winds and heavy rain.

  • Secure Property:
    • Board up glass windows, secure doors, and remove loose items like garbage cans or lumber that can fly in high winds.
  • Assemble Kit:
    • Prepare an emergency kit with a battery-operated radio, flashlights, spare batteries, non-perishable food, water, and essential medicines.
  • Safety Measures:
    • keep a list of emergency phone numbers, fill vehicles with fuel, and anchor, strengthen, or repair roofs.
  • Plan Ahead:
    • Identify the nearest sturdy shelter, locate the best route, and prepare a plan for pets.
  • Stay Indoors:
    • Remain inside, preferably in the strongest room, bathroom, or hallway away from windows.
  • Power Down:
    • Turn off electricity, gas, and water if instructed to do so.
  • Avoid Hazards:
    • Stay away from damaged buildings, fallen trees, and power lines.
  • Calm Eye:
    • Be aware that a sudden calm might mean the "eye" of the cyclone is passing; the dangerous winds will return from the opposite direction soon.

üî• Wildfire

A wildfire is an uncontrolled fire spreading through vegetation.

  • Create Defensible Space:
    • Clear dry leaves, pine needles, brush, and flammable debris from roofs, gutters, and within 100-200 feet of your home.
  • Trim Vegetation:
    • Trim branches that hang over the roof or are within 10 feet of the chimney.
  • Home Hardening:
    • Use fire-resistant materials for roofing and siding if living in high-risk areas.
  • Vehicle Safety:
    • Do not park vehicles over dry grass, as hot exhaust pipes can spark fires.
  • Evacuate Immediately:
    • Follow all evacuation orders from officials without delay.
  • Close Everything:
    • Close all windows, doors, and vents to prevent embers from entering.
  • Turn on Lights:
    • Leave lights on so your home is visible in thick smoke.
  • Wear Protective Gear:
    • Wear long sleeves, long pants, and shoes to protect against heat and flying embers.
  • Wait for Clearance:
    • Return home only when authorities declare it safe.
  • Check for Danger and Inspect Structure:
    • Look for hot spots, glowing embers, or damaged trees and power lines also check for damage to your home, specifically in the roof and attic.
